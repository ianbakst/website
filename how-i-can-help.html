How I Can Help

Three ways I typically work with a team.

Each grew out of a real problem I solved for an employer first — not a services menu written in the abstract.

0→1 Agentic Systems

Most companies that need this don't need another proof-of-concept — they need a system that survives contact with real customers, real edge cases, and real load from day one. I design and ship production AI agents: the kind that becomes the primary way customers interact with a product, not a demo that stalls out after the pilot.

Topline Pro — architected and shipped three production agents, including the primary interface ~4,000 businesses use for bookkeeping, quoting and invoicing, and inbound sales lead handling.

Fractional AI Leadership

Most companies reach the point where they need senior AI leadership well before they can justify — or fill — a full-time hire at that level. I step in part-time: setting technical direction, evaluating frameworks and vendors, establishing evaluation standards, and building the hiring pipeline for when it's time to bring the function in-house.

Topline Pro — sole AI engineer owning company-wide AI strategy, presenting and defending technical decisions to executive leadership, and designing the interview process for the company's AI engineering hires.

MLOps & Evaluation Infrastructure

An agentic system is only as trustworthy as the pipeline that deploys, evaluates, and monitors it. This is the least visible part of the work, and the part most likely to get skipped under deadline pressure — which is exactly why it matters.

Babel Street — founded the company's MLOps practice from the ground up: defined best practices, implemented MLflow, and built the deployment pipeline connecting research, product, and engineering.
