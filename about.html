About

Systems engineering, grounded in physics.

I'm an AI engineering leader with 6.5 years spanning data engineering, data science, machine learning engineering, and AI engineering. I'm currently the sole AI engineer at Topline Pro, a Series B startup, where I own AI strategy end-to-end — architecture, standards, hiring, and the roadmap connecting business goals to production agent capabilities.

Before that, I designed and built the agentic systems at the core of two products generating $50M in combined ARR at Babel Street, and founded the company's MLOps practice from scratch — the deployment pipelines, evaluation, and monitoring that make a production ML system trustworthy rather than just impressive in a demo.

My background is in mechanical and electrical engineering, capped by a Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering from Colorado State University. My research there was in computational materials science — publications on the mechanical properties of materials computed through atomistic simulation.

That combination — hands-on systems engineering plus a research background in physics-grounded computational modeling — shapes how I build: production-first, rigorously evaluated, and comfortable operating at the boundary between AI systems and hard technical domains.

I care about the same thing in both worlds. A system — whether it's a material under stress or an agent under real user load — should be tested rigorously enough that its behavior in production is not a surprise.
